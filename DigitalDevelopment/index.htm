Digital Development in CABI

Digital & Data to support the Sustainable Development Goals

Brie Finegold, Global Director, Digital Development

We support the Sustainable Development Goals and CABI’s international development work by applying digital tools to provide scientific information and evidence, promote data driven development, and support data collection and two-way communication.

How do we work?

  • We make apps, information portals, chatbots, data systems, decision support tools, computational models, and mobile phone based communication workflows
  • We offer consultancies and services to help other organisations, particuarly on FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) data and good data practice
  • We focus on the developing world
  • We work in the domains of digital tools, information, and data
  • We do geospatial and data analysis
  • We believe in user centred design and participatory appproaches
  • We follow the Principles for Digital Development

Digital Development Theme

Digital & Data to Support the Sustainable Development Goals

The Digital Development theme supports the Sustainable Development Goals and CABI’s international development work by applying digital tools to:

  • Provide scientific information and evidence to a wide variety of users
  • Promote data-driven development
  • Support data collection and two-way communication

Digital Development is a cross-cutting theme

We apply tools and skills for digital development, data, and information management to support work in CABI's technical areas (e.g. invasive species management)

We also undertake knowledge work in areas outside of CABI's technical expertise (e.g. nutrition, soil health)

Vision

CABI envisions a world in which our development impact is magnified through digital innovation in agriculture and the environment. Digital tools allow us to work with communities to overcome agricultural challenges, fight invasive species, and support resilient livelihoods and enhanced food security, while our user-centred approach helps us ensure that no one is left out of the opportunities that digital provides for economic development.

Goal 1: Two-way communication channels based on digital tools empower stakeholders, leading to better decisions, increased engagement, and improved livelihoodsl

  • Engage youth in agriculture and agribusiness through the development of digital tools
  • Combine CABI’s ICT and development communication expertise in developing approaches to digital and digitally supported agricultural extension
  • Continue to develop and support information resources & platforms , such as the Plantwise Knowledge Bank and Invasive Species Compendium,
  • Develop systems for two way communication & crowdsourcing

Goal 2: Harness the power of data to deliver solutions at scale, taking advantage of opportunities created by earth observation, sensors, and modelling, to automate analyses and generate targeted advice

  • Use geospatial tools and spatial and predictive modelling to provide tailored agricultural advice and alerts, support landscape level ecosystem management, help developing country farmers access markets, underpin climate change adaptation, and strengthen programme design and M&E.
  • Promote and build capacity for data use by partners, including data literacy, analytics tools, open data, data policy, and responsible data approaches. Undertake capacity building, consultancy, and data policy work to support good data practice across the sector.
